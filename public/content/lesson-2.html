Lesson 2

You are the Driver: The Best Way to Use AI

The "Driver" Idea

  • AI is like a powerful car engine, but you are the one holding the steering wheel.
  • The best results come from knowing your subject well, not just knowing how to "chat."

AI boosts your skills.
It cannot replace them.

The Evidence

Saving Time & Doing More

1. Saving Time

Company Reports (2024)

AI handles the "boring" work, letting researchers spend more time on new ideas and big-picture thinking.

2. The Expert Advantage

University Studies

Experts get a huge boost from AI because they know how to spot mistakes and guide the AI through tough problems.

How to Work

From "Talking" to Designing

Describing Goals

  • 1. Start with a Plan: Explain exactly what you want to achieve in simple words.
  • 2. Show, Don't Just Tell: Use your old work as a guide, not as the final template.

"Don't just ask the AI to 'move' your work. Tell it what the final goal is."

Result:

A clean, modern result instead of a messy copy-paste job.

A Warning

The Fixing Trap

Wasted Time

It's easy to waste hours trying to get AI to fix a small mistake.

The "Fixing Cycle":

"AI fixes one small thing, but breaks two other things."

Knowing When to Take Over

Success with AI is about knowing when to stop. Sometimes it is much faster to just fix the problem yourself.

The Two-Try Rule:

"If the AI doesn't solve it in two tries, do it yourself. Your own brain is still your most powerful tool."

Evidence from Git Log (askFDALabel)

40%

Of deep-logic bugs (e.g., MedDRA/Webtest) required 4+ AI attempts to stabilize.

10.5 hrs

Estimated time saved by taking over manual debugging after AI "Bug Cycles."
