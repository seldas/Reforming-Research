Lesson 1

Updating is Harder than Starting Fresh

The "Fix-it" Problem

  • Myth: AI can easily fix old projects or messy drafts.
  • Reality: AI often gets "confused" by the mistakes or limits of old work.

The "Break-One-Fix-One" Cycle

"Fixing one AI error in an old project often creates several new ones."

Advice: Sometimes it is faster to start from zero than to patch something old.

Real World Examples

Why "Starting Over" Often Wins

The Expert's Insight

Experienced users found they spent more time fixing AI suggestions for old work than if they had just let the AI build it correctly from the start.

Better Results with Clean Slates

Instead of trying to "translate" old notes or code, it's better to give AI the raw requirements. This avoids carrying over old mistakes into the new project.

New Focus: Don't be a "Repair Person" — be a "Designer."

A Personal Story

Moving from Old to New

The Goal

Moving a complex, old research tool into a modern, easy-to-use website.

The Difficulty

"A tough lesson learned."

The AI struggled to update the old logic, leading to wasted hours. Starting fresh was the only way it finally worked.

The Secret to Success

The project worked because I understood the details and knew when to stop trying to fix the old stuff.

Practical Advice:

Give the AI the rules and goals, not the old, broken version. Let it build a clean foundation for you.
